Review of a concert

At first sight – those who have seen We Have Band music videos and anticipated explosion of colours on the stage were surprised by the hanging mosquito net and just four spotlights reminding of honeycomb. At first “ear” – ‘Divisive’ and 3 tones were enough, tones of voices: Thomas, Dede and Darren. Along with guitar, sampler and drum kit they turned Dream Of A Bee (venue) into a spinning discoball with people dancing frantically like moths around a light bulb. Tempo and energy were increasing with every song (from the debut album ‘WHB’) till ‘You Came Out’ when they reached their zenith. The crowd, not even on dope, jumped up and down while whistling the chorus completely out of tune. Then during ‘Oh’ the public outshouted the band still bouncing on their feet like they would have little springs in soles of their shoes. We Have Band took a bow and left the stage but Polish audience just started to enjoy themselves. Called out by chanting coming from dozens of throats red from screaming echoing around the basement the trio played three encores and when everyone thought that ‘Honey Trap’ is the icing on the cake, the band served ‘Oh’ once more. Oh, what a great concert it was!

“We look forward to your hot, rude, full of ignorance and erudition texts” – this is how online portal elephantshoe.pl tried to encourage people to join their team. And this also reflects how their website looks like – reviews of the freshest albums and singles from the shelf labelled ‘alternative/independent music’ are preceded by editorials rich in juicy ‘fcuk’ and totally un-pc jokes about the downfall of the politics. For their first birthday they invited Gra Pozorów (Crying Game) and Coldair who adorned the covers of the two early issues of the magazine Elephant Shoe (yes, this is the moment when you go to the website and check out those pics). GP describes themselves on myspace (no, this isn’t the moment when you go to myspace and check it out) as ‘black metal’ but they’re neither very black nor metal... Although Cool Kids of Death wouldn’t abnegate them and MTV Rocks could embrace them between the playlists (if only they played Polish music of course). Coldair is a project of Tobiasz Biliński. Another project? – asked someone from the back of the room. Another one and I dare to say the best one. He no longer bows his head before Akron/Family and Animal Collective like he did in Kyst, but nods in the direction of Sam Amidon and Sufjan Stevens which turned out into ’merican” folk. And the supporting act will be performed by a band called Dog Whistle – two girls, no website... So you need to come and see these peculiarities (was supposed to be ‘personalities’, sorry): rockmen without leather jackets and long hair; a guy who likes to be photographed with a cat instead of a T-shirt; and last humans on earth without WWW address.

Text promoting an event 2

Casu marzu is a traditional Sardinian sheep milk cheese, notable for containing live insect larvae, which are a crucial part of the dish. Derived from Pecorino, casu marzu goes beyond typical fermentation to a stage most would consider decomposition, brought about by the digestive action of the larvae of the cheese fly Piophila casei. The larvae themselves appear as translucent white worms, about 8 millimetres (0.3 in) long. Casu marzu is considered to be unsafe to be eaten when the maggots in the cheese have died. Because of this, only cheese in which the maggots are still alive is usually eaten. Since the larvae in the cheese can launch themselves for distances up to 15 centimetres (6 in) when disturbed, diners hold their hands above the sandwich to prevent the maggots from leaping. Those who do not wish to eat live maggots place the cheese in a sealed paper bag. The maggots, starved for oxygen, writhe and jump in the bag, creating a "pitter-patter" sound. When the sounds subside, the maggots are dead and the cheese can be eaten. Casu marzu is believed to be an aphrodisiac by Sardinians… Casu marzu is also a title of a song by Simian Mobile Disco from their newest album ‘Delicacies’. Every music piece on this CD is named after an exotic and bizarre dainty from around the globe. Flies are attracted... Who’s seen the video to ‘Sweetbread’ knows what to eat it with! Check the ‘Delicacies’ worth 5 Michelin Stars on 18th December in 1500sqm. Served by James Ford and James Shaw.
